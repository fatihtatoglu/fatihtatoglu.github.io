Cookie Policy

tat.fatihtatoglu.com uses cookies to keep the experience consistent, plan future content, and protect the platform. This page explains what is collected, how long it is stored, and how you can manage your choices. Fatih Tatoğlu is the data controller; scope covers the blog, RSS feed, and contact channels.

We do not rely on local storage or session storage. Theme, language, and session preferences live strictly in first-party cookies.

What Is a Cookie?

Cookies are small text files that a website places in your browser. They help recognize your device, validate sessions, remember options such as theme or language, and surface analytics. Some cookies may count as personal data, so transparency is essential.

Cookies We Use

Type Example Lifetime Purpose
Essential session, language Session only Keeps the site functional; cannot be disabled.
Performance Google Analytics Up to 24 months Aggregates visit counts, clicks, and devices anonymously.
Functional Theme preference 12 months Remembers theme and language for a calmer experience.
Third-party Clarity, GTM Per provider policy Provides product insights and debugging telemetry.

First-Party Preference Cookies

  • tat-theme — stores your selected theme for 12 months.
  • tat-lang — remembers the interface language for 12 months.
  • tat-session — keeps session continuity for 2 hours, with no additional profiling.

Why We Use Cookies

  • Experience: Maintain theme, language, and content-density choices.
  • Analytics: Understand which posts resonate and prioritize new writing.
  • Security: Detect suspicious activity and prevent abuse.
  • Communication: Manage feedback forms or contact requests.

Third-Party Tools

We rely on a small toolset to interpret traffic patterns and usability:

  • Google Analytics 4: Measures visits anonymously; IPs are masked.
  • Google Tag Manager: Hosts scripts and tags without storing personal data by itself.
  • Microsoft Clarity: Generates heatmaps and session replays to discover UX issues.

Their individual privacy policies apply; please review each provider for detailed retention rules.

Managing Your Preferences

  1. Browser Settings: Most browsers allow blocking, clearing, or limiting cookies to specific domains.
  2. Private Mode: Use an incognito window when you prefer nothing to remain after the visit.
  3. Opt-out Tools: Install Google’s official GA opt-out extension if you want to block analytics globally.

Restricting cookies may impact convenience, yet core reading access stays available.

Updates & Contact
